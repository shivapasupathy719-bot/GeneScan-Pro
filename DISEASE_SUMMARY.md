# Disease Library Summary - GeneScan Pro

This document provides a summary of the hematological conditions and infections included in the GeneScan Pro disease library.

## Leukemia & Lymphoproliferative Disorders
- **Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML):** Aggressive cancer of the blood and bone marrow. Characterized by rapid growth of abnormal myeloid cells.
- **Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML):** Slow-growing cancer of the bone marrow. Often associated with the Philadelphia chromosome (BCR-ABL1).
- **Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL):** Rapidly progressing cancer of the lymphoid cells, common in children.
- **Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL):** Slow-growing cancer of the B-cells, often seen in older adults.
- **Hairy Cell Leukemia:** Rare, slow-growing leukemia with characteristic hair-like projections on the lymphocytes.
- **B-Cell Prolymphocytic Leukemia (B-PLL):** Aggressive B-cell leukemia with prominent nucleoli.
- **T-Cell Prolymphocytic Leukemia (T-PLL):** Very aggressive T-cell leukemia with rapidly rising white blood cell counts.
- **Large Granular Lymphocytic Leukemia (LGL):** Chronic leukemia of T-cells or NK-cells, often associated with rheumatoid arthritis.
- **Adult T-Cell Leukemia/Lymphoma (ATLL):** Aggressive leukemia caused by the HTLV-1 virus.
- **Plasma Cell Leukemia:** An aggressive form of multiple myeloma where plasma cells circulate in the peripheral blood.
- **Systemic Mastocytosis:** Abnormal accumulation of mast cells in various organs, often driven by the KIT D816V mutation.
- **Hypereosinophilic Syndrome (HES):** Persistent high eosinophil count causing potential organ damage.
- **Chronic Eosinophilic Leukemia:** Clonal proliferation of eosinophil precursors.
- **Chronic Neutrophilic Leukemia:** Rare leukemia characterized by a high count of mature neutrophils.
- **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD):** Lymphoma-like condition occurring after organ or stem cell transplant due to immunosuppression.
- **Castleman Disease:** Rare disorder of lymph nodes and related tissues, sometimes associated with HHV-8.
- **Rosai-Dorfman Disease:** Rare benign histiocytic disorder characterized by massive painless lymphadenopathy.
- **Langerhans Cell Histiocytosis (LCH):** Disorder where the body makes too many Langerhans cells, often causing bone lesions.
- **Chronic Myelomonocytic Leukemia (CMML):** A clonal myeloid neoplasm with features of both myelodysplastic syndromes and myeloproliferative neoplasms.
- **Burkitt Lymphoma:** A highly aggressive B-cell non-Hodgkin lymphoma with a very high proliferation rate.
- **Sézary Syndrome:** A leukemic form of cutaneous T-cell lymphoma characterized by erythroderma.
- **Waldenström Macroglobulinemia:** B-cell neoplasm characterized by bone marrow infiltration and IgM monoclonal gammopathy.

## Genetic & Inherited Disorders
- **Sickle Cell Anemia:** Inherited RBC disorder where hemoglobin S causes cells to become sickle-shaped and block blood flow.
- **Beta Thalassemia:** Genetic disorder reducing the production of beta-globin chains in hemoglobin.
- **Alpha Thalassemia:** Genetic disorder reducing the production of alpha-globin chains in hemoglobin.
- **Hereditary Spherocytosis:** Condition where RBCs are sphere-shaped, making them fragile and prone to breakdown in the spleen.
- **G6PD Deficiency:** Enzyme deficiency that causes RBCs to break down under oxidative stress (e.g., certain foods or infections).
- **Diamond-Blackfan Anemia:** Rare inherited bone marrow failure syndrome primarily affecting RBC production.
- **Fanconi Anemia:** Inherited DNA repair disorder leading to progressive bone marrow failure and physical abnormalities.
- **Polycythemia Vera:** Myeloproliferative neoplasm causing the bone marrow to make too many red blood cells.
- **Gaucher Disease (Type 1):** Lysosomal storage disorder causing accumulation of fatty substances in the spleen, liver, and bone marrow.
- **Niemann-Pick Disease:** Genetic disorder affecting lipid metabolism, leading to harmful amounts of lipids in various organs.
- **Tay-Sachs Disease:** Rare genetic disorder that causes progressive destruction of nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord.
- **Fabry Disease:** X-linked lysosomal storage disorder affecting the heart, kidneys, and skin.
- **Wolman Disease:** Severe lysosomal acid lipase deficiency causing fat accumulation in the liver, spleen, and gut.
- **Hurler Syndrome (MPS I):** Severe form of mucopolysaccharidosis type I, affecting multiple organ systems.
- **Hunter Syndrome (MPS II):** X-linked mucopolysaccharidosis type II, causing progressive physical and mental decline.
- **Sanfilippo Syndrome (MPS III):** Mucopolysaccharidosis type III primarily affecting the central nervous system.
- **Morquio Syndrome (MPS IV):** Mucopolysaccharidosis type IV primarily affecting skeletal development.
- **Maroteaux-Lamy Syndrome (MPS VI):** Mucopolysaccharidosis type VI causing skeletal and organ abnormalities.
- **Sly Syndrome (MPS VII):** Very rare mucopolysaccharidosis type VII with variable severity.
- **I-Cell Disease:** Severe lysosomal storage disorder with features similar to Hurler syndrome but more rapid progression.
- **Pompe Disease:** Glycogen storage disease type II causing progressive muscle weakness.
- **McArdle Disease:** Glycogen storage disease type V affecting the muscles' ability to use sugar.
- **Von Gierke Disease:** Glycogen storage disease type Ia causing severe hypoglycemia and liver enlargement.
- **Cori Disease:** Glycogen storage disease type III affecting liver and muscle tissues.
- **Anderson Disease:** Glycogen storage disease type IV causing progressive liver cirrhosis.
- **Hers Disease:** Glycogen storage disease type VI, a milder form of glycogen storage disease affecting the liver.
- **Tarui Disease:** Glycogen storage disease type VII causing exercise-induced muscle pain and hemolytic anemia.
- **Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (TTP):** Rare blood disorder characterized by the formation of small clots throughout the body, consuming platelets.

## Anemias & RBC Disorders
- **Iron Deficiency Anemia:** The most common type of anemia, caused by a lack of iron for hemoglobin production.
- **Vitamin B12 Deficiency Anemia:** Macrocytic anemia caused by insufficient B12, often due to dietary lack or absorption issues.
- **Folate Deficiency Anemia:** Macrocytic anemia similar to B12 deficiency, caused by a lack of folic acid.
- **Aplastic Anemia:** A condition where the bone marrow stops producing enough new blood cells of all types.
- **Autoimmune Hemolytic Anemia:** A condition where the immune system mistakenly produces antibodies that attack and destroy red blood cells.
- **Secondary Polycythemia (Hypoxia):** Increased red blood cell production as a physiological response to low oxygen levels (e.g., high altitude, lung disease).
- **Secondary Polycythemia (Tumor):** Increased red blood cell production caused by erythropoietin-secreting tumors.
- **Relative Polycythemia:** An apparent increase in red blood cell concentration due to decreased plasma volume (dehydration).
- **Gaisböck Syndrome:** A form of relative polycythemia often associated with stress, hypertension, and obesity.

## Infections & Immune Syndromes
- **Malaria:** A life-threatening disease caused by parasites transmitted through the bites of infected mosquitoes, infecting red blood cells.
- **Infectious Mononucleosis:** A viral infection (usually EBV) characterized by fever, sore throat, and atypical lymphocytes on a blood smear.
- **Dengue Fever:** A mosquito-borne viral infection that can cause severe flu-like symptoms and a drop in platelet counts.
- **Sepsis:** A life-threatening emergency that happens when the body's response to an infection damages its own tissues.
- **Hemophagocytic Lymphohistiocytosis (HLH):** A severe, life-threatening systemic inflammatory syndrome characterized by overactive immune cells.
- **Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC):** A serious condition where the blood's clotting mechanism is overactivated, leading to both clots and bleeding.
